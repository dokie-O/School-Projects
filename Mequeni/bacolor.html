BACOLOR

“ATHENS OF PAMPANGA”

EXPLORE BACOLOR

Bacolor, a municipality in the province of Pampanga, Philippines, is a place rich in history and culture. Known for its resilience after being heavily affected by the eruption of Mount Pinatubo in 1991, Bacolor has managed to preserve its cultural heritage and continues to thrive.

BRIEF HISTORY

Bacolor, located in Pampanga, Philippines, has a rich history dating back to its early settlement in 1571. Originally named "Baculud" due to its elevated terrain, it was later renamed Bacolor upon the arrival of the Spanish. The town's founding in 1574 centered around the San Guillermo Church, built on the estate of Guillermo Manabat.

BRIEF HISTORY

Bacolor’s history took a tragic turn with the eruption of Mount Pinatubo in 1991, which buried much of the town under 20 feet of lahar. This natural disaster devastated 18 out of Bacolor’s 21 barangays, dramatically altering its landscape and impacting its community.
