LUBAO

"BALEN BABA"

EXPLORE LUBAO

Lubao, a municipality in the province of Pampanga, Philippines, is rich in history, culture, and natural beauty. Known as the birthplace of two Philippine Presidents, Lubao offers a blend of historical landmarks, cultural experiences, and scenic spots.

BRIEF HISTORY

Lubao, known as "BABA" and "LUBAO" by early Kapampangan settlers, signifies its low-lying geographical features, playing a significant role in the cosmological and epochal development of the town as the Cradle of the Kapampangan Kingdom. With roots traced back to the lineage of Rajah Soliman, Lubao's political leadership thrived under the descendants of the powerful Soliman clans.

BRIEF HISTORY

Prospering as a kingdom prior to Spanish arrival, Lubao boasted an organized government led by Datu Macabulus and a Council of Elders in Balas (now Sta. Cruz). Today, Lubao remains a first-class municipality with a rich agricultural landscape, contributing significantly to Pampanga's rice production, while its historical and cultural heritage earn it the title of the Cradle of the Kapampangan Civilization.
