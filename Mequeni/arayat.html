ARAYAT

"THE LEGENDARY MOUNTAIN TOWN"

EXPLORE ARAYAT

Arayat is a municipality in the province of Pampanga, Philippines, known for its natural beauty, historical sites, and vibrant local culture. Dominated by the majestic Mount Arayat, this area offers a mix of outdoor adventures, cultural experiences, and scenic landscapes.

BRIEF HISTORY

Arayat is one of the oldest towns in Pampanga, Central Luzon, with its name likely derived from a native word meaning "to the east." The town is renowned for its majestic Mt. Arayat, also known as Mt. Sinukuan after the legendary Kapampangan god, Aring Sinukuan. Arayat's rich history dates back to the 14th to 16th centuries, well before the Spanish conquest

BRIEF HISTORY

1571, Arayat was already considered to be one of the most progressive Spanish settlements in Pampanga. In 1590, the first Agustinian mission was established in the town by Fray Juan de Valderama.

Arayat became a Spanish military fort in 1600. By 1853, records show that it consisted of 11 barangays (barrios), 23 barangays in 1903. The number of barangays has increased to 30 as of the present time.
