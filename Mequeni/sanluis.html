SAN LUIS

"PLACE FULL OF FRUIT BAT"

EXPLORE SAN LUIS

San Luis has a predominantly agricultural economy, with rice farming as the mainstay. The local government has been focusing on improving infrastructure and supporting local industries to boost the economy. Efforts are also being made to promote eco-tourism and cultural heritage preservation.

BRIEF HISTORY

San Luis town was originally called Cabagsa or Cabagsac, a contraction of “cabag” (fruit bats) and “bagsac”, hence a place where fruit bats come to roost. To honor the memory of one of its cura, Fray Nicolas de Orduno, it was later known as San Nicolas de Cabagsa. The eventual name—San Luis—is attributed to Dna. Luisa, wife of a lawyer who acted as a counsel in claiming a parcel of land disputed by neighboring Santa Ana (then Pinpin) town.

BRIEF HISTORY

San Luis is located in the low lying areas Southeast of Pampanga. South of the Municipality are the areas whose physiography is gently sloping and flood does occur. It is a highly agricultural town where the dominant crop is palay, followed by vegetables and orchards, respectively. Agri businesses like inland fish culture, poultry, piggery and other livestock raising is noted. It serves as a satellite municipality of Pampanga urban centers, being a major supplier of food.
