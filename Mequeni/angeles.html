ANGELES

"THE CITY OF ANGELS"

EXPLORE ANGELES

Angeles City is one of the highly urbanized cities in Pampanga, known for its vibrant culture, rich history, and dynamic lifestyle. Located in the central part of the province, Angeles City is a bustling urban center that offers a mix of historical sites, modern attractions, and diverse dining and entertainment options.

BRIEF HISTORY

Angeles, a city in the Philippines, derives its name from the Spanish "El Pueblo de los Ángeles," meaning "The Town of the Angels," honoring its saints and founder, Don Angel Pantaleón de Miranda. Founded in 1796, Angeles boasts significant historical landmarks such as the Holy Rosary Church and Museo Ning Angeles, which offer insights into the city's rich cultural heritage. The Pamintuan Mansion, a historic building that has served various roles throughout history, and the Founders' Residence, the original home of Don Angel, are notable attractions.

BRIEF HISTORY

To the west of the city lies Mount Pinatubo, an active volcano renowned for its 1991 eruption and now a popular site for trekking and exploring its crater lake. Although part of Pampanga Province, Angeles is politically independent and classified as a highly urbanized city in Central Luzon.

This status grants it administrative and fiscal autonomy, enhancing its urban development. In a 2008 survey by MoneySense magazine, Angeles ranked 15th among the "Best Places to Live in the Philippines," reflecting its quality of life. The city seamlessly blends historical charm with modern convenience, making it a vibrant and appealing destination.
