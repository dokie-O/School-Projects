SAN FERNANDO

"CAPITAL OF PAMPANGA"

EXPLORE SAN FERNANDO

San Fernando, the capital city of Pampanga, is a vibrant urban center known for its rich cultural heritage, historical landmarks, and festive atmosphere. Located in the heart of Central Luzon, San Fernando is a significant hub for commerce, industry, and tourism in the region.

BRIEF HISTORY

San Fernando, a city in Pampanga, derives its name from King Ferdinand VI of Spain and honors Saint Ferdinand III of Castile and León, celebrated annually on May 30. Renowned as the "Christmas Capital of the Philippines," it hosts the Giant Lantern Festival each December, earning the title 'Asia's Christmas capital' by CNN. Established in 1874 as the valley’s first organized community, it earned the moniker “First City of the Valley,” with its growth spurred by the arrival of the railroad in 1876.

BRIEF HISTORY

Today, San Fernando seamlessly blends its agricultural roots near the Mission de San Fernando Rey with its transformation into a vibrant commercial and industrial center. Residents enjoy modern amenities, a strong community spirit, efficient city services, accessible government, a plentiful labor pool, lower business taxes compared to Los Angeles, and no utility taxes, making it an attractive destination for both residents and businesses seeking a dynamic yet welcoming community.
