MACABEBE

"BANKS OF RIO GRANDE DE PAMPANGA"

EXPLORE MACABEBE

Macabebe offers a blend of historical charm, cultural richness, and natural beauty. From the historic St. Nicolas de Tolentino Parish Church and well-preserved heritage houses to the vibrant town fiestas and delicious Kapampangan cuisine, Macabebe provides a diverse and enriching experience for visitors.

BRIEF HISTORY

Macabebe, known for its role in the resistance against Spanish colonization, is one of Pampanga's oldest towns, dating back to pre-colonial times. The town is proud of its rich fishing industry, with the annual "Masaman" Festival celebrating its bountiful harvests from the river.

BRIEF HISTORY

The festival features various activities, including boat races and fishing competitions, reflecting the community's deep connection to the water. Macabebe is also home to the revered San Nicolas de Tolentino Parish Church, a historical landmark in the town.
