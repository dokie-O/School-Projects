STO. TOMAS

"COFFIN CAPITAL OF CENTRAL LUZON"

EXPLORE STO. TOMAS

Sto. Tomas, Pampanga, is renowned for its pottery industry. The town's rich tradition of pottery-making dates back centuries, and it continues to be a significant part of its cultural heritage and economic activity. Sto. Tomas is home to skilled artisans who craft a variety of earthenware products, including pots, jars, vases, and decorative items, using traditional methods passed down through generations.

BRIEF HISTORY

On September 15, 1792, Santo Tomas was severed from its parent Minalin, PampangaOn May 4, 1899, the town was under the U.S. Force's administration. On January 2, 1905, it was ceded to san fernando, Pampanga until 1905. As the youngest and smallest Pampanga town, Santo Tomas has a total land area of 1,467 hectares (3,625 acres). It is a mainly agricultural and fishing community; industry includes casket manufacture, ceramics, and carpentry
