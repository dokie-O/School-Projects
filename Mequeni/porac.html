PORAC

"LARGEST TOWN IN PAMPANGA"

EXPLORE PORAC

Porac is one of the oldest towns in Pampanga, established in 1594. Its name is derived from the word "purac," a type of plant that used to grow abundantly in the area. The town has a rich history that spans the Spanish colonial period, American occupation, and contemporary times.

BRIEF HISTORY

Founded in 1594, Porac is known for its scenic landscapes and the Mount Pinatubo trek. The municipality is renowned for its delicious "tocino" (cured pork) and hosts the "Binulu Festival," celebrating its indigenous cooking method using bamboo tubes.

BRIEF HISTORY

This festival includes cooking demonstrations and a showcase of traditional dishes prepared in bamboo. Porac's natural attractions, such as the Miyamit Falls and Puning Hot Springs, make it a popular destination for eco-tourism and adventure seekers.
