GUAGUA

"RIVER MOUTH"

EXPLORE GUAGUA

Guagua is a municipality in the province of Pampanga, Philippines, known for its rich cultural heritage, historical landmarks, and vibrant local culture. Situated in the heart of Central Luzon, Guagua offers visitors a variety of attractions and activities to explore.

BRIEF HISTORY

Guagua, formerly known as "Wawa," traces its origins to a Hindu-Malayan term, possibly meaning "the mouth of a river," which later evolved into its present name. Founded as a town in 1590, Guagua became a significant settlement along water routes, strategically located near the Rio Grande (Pampanga River) in Pampanga.

BRIEF HISTORY

With a land area predominantly dedicated to agriculture, Guagua thrives on crops like palay, sugar, and vegetables, alongside burgeoning industries such as ice plants, woodcraft, and retail trade businesses. Notably, Guagua boasts the largest public market in Pampanga, drawing visitors from neighboring towns, while initiatives like sampaguita and ilang-ilang cultivation enhance the town's economic landscape and uplift the local community.
