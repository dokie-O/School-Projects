BRIEF HISTORY

Mabalacat, Pampanga, reflects the diverse heritage of many Philippine cities. Originally home to an Aeta tribe, the area was known for its abundance of balacat trees, giving it the name "full of Balacats" in the local Kapampangan language. Over time, its population grew from 2,611 residents in 1853 to 7,049 in 1903, expanding to 20 barangays by 1948. During the Spanish era, Mabalacat experienced military intervention due to lawlessness, particularly by the Aetas, alongside significant involvement from friars like Fr. Andres de San Fulgencio and Fr. Gregorio Bueno. .

BRIEF HISTORY

The Spanish friars meticulously documented the region's history, leaving behind extensive records that offer valuable insights into Mabalacat's past, now preserved in archives locally and abroad. Today, Mabalacat features attractions like Nayong Pilipino Clark, Clark Freeport Zone, Aqua Planet, and Zoocobia Fun Zoo, showcasing its cultural heritage and modern amenities. For adventure seekers, Mount Pinatubo and the Clark Museum provide unique experiences, while the Mabalacat Public Market offers an authentic local shopping experience.
