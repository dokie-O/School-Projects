APALIT

"THE BLACKSMITH CAPITAL OF PAMPANGA"

EXPLORE APALIT

Apalit, a municipality in the province of Pampanga, Philippines, is known for its rich cultural heritage, historical landmarks, and vibrant local festivals. Situated in the southern part of Pampanga, Apalit offers visitors a blend of historical sites, religious traditions, and scenic spots.

BRIEF HISTORY

Apalit, founded in 1597, located in Pampanga, Philippines, beckons visitors with its rich history and dynamic economy. Named after the indigenous Apalit tree, Pterocarpus Indicus, the town is steeped in cultural significance. Tourists can marvel at the iconic St. Peter the Apostle Parish Church, renowned for its Baroque architecture, and join the annual Apung Iru Fluvial Procession along the picturesque Pampanga River. Industrial aficionados can explore the Philippine Toyota Plant, while culinary enthusiasts can indulge in local delicacies at Mekeni Food Corporation.

BRIEF HISTORY

The town's markets and metal craft shops offer unique handcrafted items, reflecting its artisanal heritage. Nature lovers can escape to the rural outskirts, exploring hiking trails amidst tranquil countryside vistas. Throughout the year, Apalit hosts vibrant festivals and cultural events, offering insights into its lively traditions. Historical sites and monuments add further depth, commemorating pivotal moments and figures in Apalit's storied past.
