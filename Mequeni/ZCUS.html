Balo balo, also called burung bulig, is a unique dish from Candaba, Pampanga. It’s made by fermenting mudfish with rice, creating a strong-flavored and acquired-taste food item. Due to the preparation process, it’s not commonly found and often enjoyed during festivities in the region.

Duman rice is a prized seasonal treat from Santa Rita, Pampanga. Made from leftover glutinous rice kernels clinging to stalks after harvest, it involves beating the husks to release the precious “lakatan malutu” grains. This laborious process results in a unique, golden-green rice enjoyed for its special flavor and cultural significance.

Betute tugak, or stuffed frogs, is a specialty dish in Mexico, Pampanga, and possibly Magalang as well. While the Tugak Festival (frog festival) is celebrated in San Fernando, the true origin of betute tugak might be in Mexico or Magalang. This dish highlights the unique culinary traditions of Pampanga and the resourcefulness of using local ingredients.

Adobong kamaru is a fascinating dish enjoyed throughout Pampanga. This “adobo” features mole crickets instead of the usual meat, stir-fried with vinegar and garlic. The result is a crunchy-on-the-outside, gooey-on-the-inside treat, considered an adventurous local delicacy.

While “Pampanga’s Best” is likely a brand name, Pampanga itself is renowned for its tocino and carabao (water buffalo) meat products. These include tocino (sweet cured pork) and pindang (a type of cured meat). The sweet and savory flavors of Pampanga’s Best products are beloved by locals and sought after by visitors.

Tocino and pindang are two signature products of Pampanga’s Best, a renowned brand hailing from San Fernando, Pampanga. Tocino is a sweetened and cured pork dish often served for breakfast, while pindang refers to various types of cured meats, typically made from beef or carabao. These products reflect the culinary heritage and quality ingredients of Pampanga.
