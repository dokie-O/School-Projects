FLORIDABLANCA

"3RD LARGEST MUNICIPALITY"

EXPLORE FLORIDABLANCA

Floridablanca, a municipality in the province of Pampanga, Philippines, offers a mix of historical charm, natural beauty, and vibrant local culture. Known for its scenic landscapes and historical landmarks, Floridablanca provides visitors with a range of activities and attractions.

BRIEF HISTORY

Floridablanca, originally established in 1823 as the Hacienda de San Jose de Calampaui under Spanish friars, later became known as "Pueblo de Floridablanca" in 1867. The town's name, meaning "white flower" in Spanish, reflects its scenic beauty and possibly its abundant floral landscape.

BRIEF HISTORY

Throughout its history, Floridablanca has seen significant developments, including the establishment of the Pampanga Sugar Mill (PASUMIL) in the 1920s, contributing to the town's economic growth as a major sugar manufacturer. Additionally, the Philippine Air Force's Basa Air Base, established in 1947 on an American military airfield, further solidified Floridablanca's strategic importance in the region.
