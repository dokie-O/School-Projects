Ideal time to visit Pampanga

Pampanga, like the rest of the Philippines, has a wet and a dry season. The hottest months are from March to May, while the coolest are from December to February. The best time to visit is from November to February, during the dry and cool months, when the Giant Lantern Festival takes place in December. If you visit during Holy Week in March or April, you can see the intense Easter crucifixion ceremonies, where devotees reenact Jesus' crucifixion. Check the weather forecast before planning your trip.

How to Get to Pampanga

Pampanga is easily accessible from Manila by car, taking only about 2 to 3 hours to reach Angeles City. Many bus lines also service the area, stopping in San Fernando, Angeles City, and Mabalacat City. Alternatively, you can fly to Pampanga via Clark International Airport. Here's how you can get to Pampanga.


By Land Transport


From Manila via Private Transport:
Drive to Pampanga via the North Luzon Expressway (NLEX).
- Take the San Fernando Exit for the provincial capital, near SM City Pampanga and Robinsons Starmall Pampanga.
- Take the Angeles Exit to reach Angeles City and the Clark Freeport Zone, near Marquee Mall.


By Commuter Bus:


- Travel from Manila to Angeles City, about 2-3 hours by bus.
- Major bus companies like Victory Liner, Five Stars, Philippine Rabbit, and Philtranco have frequent trips from Pasay and Cubao to Dau Bus Terminal in Angeles.
- From Dau Bus Terminal, take a jeepney or taxi to your destination in Pampanga.


By P2P Bus:


- Premium Point-to-Point buses connect NAIA in Manila to Clark International Airport.
- Pickup points include NAIA Terminal 3, Robinsons Galleria in Ortigas, and TRINOMA Mall in Quezon City.


By Air


From Cebu City:

- Philippine Airlines, Cebu Pacific, and AirAsia offer daily flights to Clark International Airport.
- Flight time is approximately 1 hour and 30 minutes.

From Davao City:

- Philippine Airlines and Cebu Pacific fly four times daily, while AirAsia flies once daily to Clark International Airport.
- Total travel time is about 1 hour and 50 minutes.

From International Airports:

- Major airlines from Qatar, UAE, Hong Kong, South Korea, and Singapore have daily flights to Clark International Airport.
- Alternatively, fly to NAIA in Manila and take a car or bus to Pampanga.

Getting Around Pampanga

- Use jeepneys, tricycles, taxis, and UV Express shuttles for local travel.
- Book tours with transfers for convenience.
- From Clark Airport, hire airport transfers or rent a car. For larger groups, consider van rentals. For more options, book self-drive car rentals, including budget, SUV, or luxury cars.

Do's and Don'ts in travelling Pampanga


Do's

- Do try the local cuisine:
Pampanga is known as the culinary capital of the Philippines. Make sure to try popular dishes like sisig, tocino, and bringhe.

- Do visit cultural sites:
Explore historical and cultural sites such as the San Guillermo Church, Mt. Arayat National Park, and the Clark Museum.

- Do attend festivals:
Experience local festivals like the Giant Lantern Festival in December and the Sinukwan Festival.

- Do respect local customs:
Be polite and courteous to locals. Respect their traditions and religious practices.

- Do plan your trip during the dry season:
The best time to visit is from November to February, when the weather is cooler and drier.

Don'ts:

- Don't Litter:
Keep the province clean by disposing of trash properly.

- Don't Miss the Festivals:
Experience local culture by attending events like the Giant Lantern Festival in December.

- Don't Disregard Dress Codes:
Dress modestly when visiting churches and religious sites.

- Don't Neglect Safety Precautions:
Be mindful of your belongings and avoid unsafe areas, especially at night.

- Don't Haggle Aggressively:
When shopping in local markets, negotiate prices politely and respectfully.
