CANDABA

“ECOTOURISM CAPITAL OF PAMPANGA”

EXPLORE CANDABA

Candaba is a municipality in the province of Pampanga, Philippines, renowned for its rich agricultural lands, vibrant wildlife, and unique cultural heritage. Nestled in the heart of Central Luzon, Candaba offers a mix of natural beauty, traditional festivities, and historical landmarks.

BRIEF HISTORY

Candaba, a municipality in Pampanga, Philippines, holds a rich history dating back to pre-Hispanic times, characterized by indigenous farming and fishing traditions. Its name, likely derived from "Candawe" or "Cang Daba," reflects its deep-rooted heritage and cultural significance.

BRIEF HISTORY

During the Spanish period, Candaba emerged as a vital missionary center for Augustinian Friars, with the San Andres Apostol Church standing as a testament to its religious influence. Today, the town's ecological importance is highlighted by the Candaba Swamp, a crucial habitat for migratory birds and a hub for biodiversity, contributing to its unique charm and appeal.
