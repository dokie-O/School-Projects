MASANTOL

"THE FISHING CAPITAL OF PAMPANGA"

EXPLORE MASANTOL

Masantol is a charming municipality in the province of Pampanga, Philippines, known for its serene river views, cultural heritage, and vibrant local community. Situated along the Pampanga River, Masantol offers a mix of historical, cultural, and natural attractions.

BRIEF HISTORY

Established in 1877, Masantol is known for its vast fishponds and the bustling fishing community. The town takes pride in its seafood, particularly the famed "Masantol Alamang" (shrimp paste), and celebrates the "Alauli Festival," highlighting its fishing heritage.

BRIEF HISTORY

The festival also features cultural presentations and a parade showcasing the town's rich history and traditions. Masantol is also famous for its delicious "Masantol Sweets," which include a variety of native delicacies enjoyed by visitors.
